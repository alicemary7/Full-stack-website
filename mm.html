Menarche

  • Menarche is the first occurrence of menstruation — it marks the beginning of a girl’s reproductive years.
  • It usually happens between the ages of 9 and 15, depending on factors like genetics, nutrition, and overall health
  • Along with bleeding, girls may experience cramps, mood swings, breast development, and body changes as part of puberty
  • Menarche is an important biological and social milestone, symbolizing the transition from childhood to adolescence.

Menstrual Cycle

  • The menstrual cycle is the monthly process in which a woman’s body prepares for a possible pregnancy — involving hormonal changes, ovulation, and menstruation.
  • A full cycle usually lasts about 28 days, but it can range from 21 to 35 days, depending on the individual.
  • It has four phases — Menstrual phase (bleeding), Follicular phase (egg develops), Ovulation phase (egg released), and Luteal phase (uterus prepares for pregnancy).
  • The menstrual cycle helps the body prepare for reproduction — if no fertilization occurs, the uterine lining sheds, leading to a period.

Menopause

  • Menopause is the natural end of a woman’s menstrual cycles, marking the end of her reproductive years.
  • It typically occurs between the ages of 45 and 55, though it can vary depending on health, lifestyle, and genetics.
  • Common symptoms include hot flashes, mood swings, irregular periods, night sweats, and sleep disturbances due to hormonal changes.
  • Menopause signals a major life transition, not an illness — it’s a normal part of aging that can also bring new balance and freedom from menstrual cycles.
